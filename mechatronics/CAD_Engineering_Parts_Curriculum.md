# CAD Engineering Parts Curriculum

This guide is organized from simple geometry to complete mechanisms. The
goal is to master one building block at a time before designing complex
machines.

# Level 2 -- Mechanical Fastening

-   **Bolt** -- Threaded fastener. Learn threads and clearances. Used
    everywhere.
-   **Nut** -- Works with bolts to clamp parts together.
-   **Threaded Rod** -- Long threaded shaft for adjustable assemblies.
-   **Thumb Screw** -- Hand-tightened screw for tool-free adjustments.
-   **Dowel Pin** -- Precision alignment pin for repeatable positioning.
-   **Clevis Pin** -- Pin with retaining clip for pivot joints.
-   **Cotter Pin** -- Locks fasteners in place.
-   **Retaining Ring** -- Keeps components from sliding off shafts.
-   **Snap Fit** -- Flexible locking feature molded into a part.
-   **Press-Fit Peg** -- Friction-fit connection without hardware.

Skills: tolerances, fits, fastening, assemblies.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Level 3 -- Motion Components

-   Spur Gear -- Simple gear for parallel shafts.
-   Helical Gear -- Smoother, quieter angled gear.
-   Bevel Gear -- Transfers motion between intersecting shafts.
-   Worm Gear -- High reduction with self-locking capability.
-   Rack and Pinion -- Converts rotation to linear motion.
-   Pulley -- Transfers motion with a belt.
-   Timing Pulley -- Toothed pulley for precise positioning.
-   Belt Tensioner -- Maintains belt tension.
-   Sprocket -- Drives chains.
-   Chain Link -- Individual link in a chain system.
-   Hinge -- Allows rotation between two parts.
-   Spring -- Stores mechanical energy.
-   Leaf Spring -- Flat flexible spring.
-   Torsion Spring -- Twists to store energy.
-   Carabiner -- Spring-loaded clip.
-   Cam -- Converts rotation into custom motion.
-   Crank -- Converts rotary to reciprocating motion.
-   Slider -- Moves in a straight path.
-   Connecting Rod -- Links rotating and sliding parts.

Skills: kinematics, power transmission, mechanical motion.

# Recommended Learning Path

1.  Master Level 1 until every model feels easy.
2.  Add hardware with Level 2.
3.  Build moving parts in Levels 3--4.
4.  Design complete structures in Levels 5--6.
5.  Learn power systems and motion with Levels 7--8.
6.  Integrate electronics in Level 9.
7.  Build complete machines in Levels 10--11.

**Tip:** Don't just model the parts---assemble them. Learning how
components interact is where CAD becomes engineering.
